**1/8**  
Silent.  
Yet the ten thousand things arise, shift, and return without cease.  
Nothing possesses, yet everything is carried forward.

**2/8**  
Heaven’s way leaves no trace, yet moves through all.  
Earth bears form without clinging to any one shape.  
Between them, human beings stand — where the ceaseless arising finds its own heart.

**3/8**  
Resting in Heaven’s unbound nature, one does not fear the open.  
Standing within Earth’s clear limits, one feels the full weight of what appears — and what will pass.  
This feeling, this concern, is not a flaw. It is how humanity becomes real.

**4/8**  
Do not chase borrowed names or positions outside.  
Do not dissolve into emptiness to escape the weight.  
Stay exactly at the boundary of what is given — and bear it.

**5/8**  
Life is bestowed from Heaven, yet must be taken up by one’s own body and heart.  
Only through fully bearing what is finite does the deeper movement begin.  
Here, all suffering is born from clinging to the open,  
and all openness shows its face only through what must be borne.

**6/8**  
Heaven and Earth do not speak.  
They simply give birth and take back.  
The human heart, when unobscured, becomes its own light —  
not by turning away from form, but by carrying it completely.

**7/8**  
No fleeing outward for status.  
No hiding in the formless to avoid pain.  
Only this: stand here, take it up, moment after moment.

**8/8**  
Then, in a single genuine movement of compassion —  
raw, uncalculated, arising right within the weight —  
the living pulse of Heaven flashes through the human heart.  

One does not reach Heaven by leaving the world.  
One lets Heaven appear by fully standing in it. 🍂
